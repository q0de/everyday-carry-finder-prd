# Everyday Carry Finder PRD v5

Updated: June 20, 2026

Status: canonical build-ready PRD

## 1. Executive Summary

Everyday Carry Finder is a practical recommendation site for people who want an everyday carry setup that fits their real life: budget, pocket space, work rules, travel needs, knife preference, and comfort level.

The MVP is a low-cost validation test, not a full app. Build six static SEO pages first, track indexing and outbound product clicks, then decide whether to expand.

Canonical product thesis:

> Help people find the right everyday carry setup for their life, budget, and constraints.

The product should not be positioned as a knife-first site, a tactical site, or a no-knife-only site. No-knife and TSA-friendly carry are important lanes inside a broader practical EDC product.

## 2. Current Decision

Build Phase A only.

Phase A is a 6-page static SEO MVP:

1. `/edc-checklist/` - Everyday Carry Checklist
2. `/best-edc-gear-for-beginners/` - Best EDC Gear for Beginners
3. `/best-edc-kit-under-100/` - Best EDC Kit Under $100
4. `/tsa-friendly-edc-kit/` - TSA-Friendly EDC Kit
5. `/best-keychain-edc/` - Best Keychain EDC
6. `/best-edc-without-a-knife/` - Best EDC Without a Knife

Do not build the Carry Builder quiz, freshness automation, user accounts, or a custom product admin in Phase A.

## 3. Target Users

Primary launch users:

- EDC beginners who want a useful starter setup without learning the whole category.
- Budget-conscious buyers comparing practical kits under about $100.
- Travelers who need conservative TSA-friendly guidance.
- Office and urban carriers who want compact, non-tactical gear.
- Keychain-focused users who want small useful tools without pocket clutter.
- People who do not want to carry a knife, or cannot carry one in their normal context.

Deferred users:

- Knife collectors.
- Tactical/survival-prepper audiences.
- Premium flashlight enthusiasts.
- Trade-specific users like electricians or EMTs.
- Community loadout sharers.

## 4. Positioning

Positioning statement:

> Everyday Carry Finder helps people build practical carry setups by budget, lifestyle, pocket space, and travel/work constraints.

Voice and brand feel:

- Practical.
- Edited.
- Trustworthy.
- Beginner-friendly.
- Honest about tradeoffs.
- Not tactical or survivalist.
- Not generic affiliate content.

Avoid:

- Knife-first framing.
- No-knife-only framing.
- Tactical/survival framing.
- Giant undifferentiated "top 25 gear" lists.
- Unsupported legal, TSA, or workplace safety claims.
- Recommendations without a reason.

## 5. Success Definition

The first validation goal is not profit. It is evidence that Google understands the site and that users click product links.

Phase A is successful if, within 120-180 days after publication, at least two are true:

- Three or more pages receive repeated Google Search Console impressions.
- At least one page receives organic clicks.
- Tracked outbound product clicks occur from multiple pages.
- One content cluster clearly outperforms the others.
- Direct affiliate opportunities become realistic because the site has useful content and early traction.

Pivot if:

- Broad beginner/budget pages fail but TSA/no-knife pages show impressions.
- Informational pages get impressions but buyer pages do not.
- Keychain or budget pages get clicks but weak monetization.
- The quiz concept receives interest, but product clicks remain weak.

Park the project if:

- Pages do not index after technical fixes.
- SERPs are unwinnable across all Phase A topics.
- There are no outbound clicks after meaningful impressions.
- Content freshness and product testing burden exceeds expected upside.

## 6. Phase Plan

### Phase A: Static SEO MVP

Build six static pages, product data, affiliate disclosures, basic analytics, tracked outbound links, sitemap, and Search Console setup.

### Phase B: Expansion Pages

Add only after Phase A shows signal:

- `/best-office-edc/`
- `/best-travel-edc/`
- `/best-minimalist-edc/`
- `/best-edc-kit-under-50/`
- `/pocket-edc-vs-pouch-edc/`

### Phase C: Carry Builder Quiz

Build the quiz only after at least one is true:

- Three or more pages are indexed and getting repeated impressions.
- At least 50 tracked affiliate/product outbound clicks have occurred.
- Search Console reveals a clear winning cluster.
- Product data has enough stable IDs and tags to power deterministic recommendations.

The quiz should map users to existing pages and product sets. It should not require a full recommendation engine.

## 8. Keyword And SERP Validation

No page should be drafted until its keyword/SERP row is completed.

Required fields:

| Field | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Page URL | Phase A URL |
| Primary keyword | Main query |
| Secondary keywords | Supporting queries |
| Search intent | Informational, commercial investigation, transactional, mixed |
| Search volume estimate | Use available SEO tool, browser research, or clearly marked manual estimate |
| Keyword difficulty estimate | Use available tool or manual low/medium/high |
| Top 5 ranking URLs | Current competitors |
| Competitor type | Reddit, YouTube, publisher, ecommerce, forum, brand, AI/overview |
| SERP weakness | The gap this page can exploit |
| AI/zero-click risk | Low, medium, high |
| Affiliate fit | Low, medium, high |
| Decision | Build, reframe, defer, drop |

Phase A cannot begin content production until all six rows are filled and at least four pages remain "build."

## 9. Product Data Model

Phase A may use a CSV, spreadsheet, Airtable, or JSON file. Do not build a custom admin in Phase A.

Required fields:

```text

brand

price_band

launch_page_fit
tags

last_price_checked_at
last_availability_checked_at
recommendation_status
notes
```

Allowed `tested_status` values:

- `tested`
- `handled`
- `researched`

Allowed `recommendation_status` values:

- `candidate`
- `approved`
- `rejected`
- `needs_review`
- `watch`
- `removed`

Minimum Phase A product seed:

- 6-8 wallets/card holders.
- 6-8 compact flashlights.
- 6-8 pens.
- 5-7 key organizers/keychain tools.
- 5-7 pouches/organizers.
- 4-6 chargers/cables/power accessories.
- 4-6 TSA-friendly/no-knife tools.
- 4-6 optional knives for non-TSA pages only if broader pages need them.

Minimum before publishing:

- At least 35 candidate products.
- At least 5 recommendations per Phase A page.
- At least 3 personally tested or handled products.
- Clear `tested`, `handled`, or `researched` label for every recommendation.

Product exclusion rules:

- Exclude unavailable products.
- Exclude products with broken affiliate/canonical URLs.
- Exclude products that violate a page constraint.
- Exclude products that only fit because of vague "cool gear" appeal.
- Exclude products with repeated credible quality or safety complaints unless clearly marked as rejected/watch.

## 12. Analytics Spec

Analytics can use Plausible, PostHog, GA4, or another lightweight tool. The implementation must support event names and properties below.

Events:

- `affiliate_click`
- `product_card_click`
- `comparison_table_click`
- `internal_related_page_click`
- `page_cta_click`
- `quiz_start`
- `quiz_complete`
- `quiz_product_click`

Phase A requires only the non-quiz events.

Required properties:

```text
page_slug
page_type
product_id
product_category
merchant
affiliate_program
price_band
position_on_page
cta_label
recommendation_context
is_affiliate
```

Example event:

```json
{
  "event": "affiliate_click",
  "page_slug": "tsa-friendly-edc-kit",
  "page_type": "constraint_buyer",
  "product_id": "flashlight_001",
  "product_category": "flashlight",
  "merchant": "amazon",
  "affiliate_program": "amazon_associates",
  "price_band": "$15-$30",
  "position_on_page": 3,
  "cta_label": "Check price",
  "recommendation_context": "compact flashlight",
  "is_affiliate": true
}
```

Weekly review dashboard:

- GSC impressions by page.
- GSC clicks by page.
- Outbound clicks by page.
- Outbound clicks by product.
- Click-through rate from page view to product click.
- Top internal link clicks.
- Products with clicks but weak availability or affiliate fit.

## 13. Compliance

Required standard affiliate disclosure:

> Some links on this page may be affiliate links. If you buy through them, we may earn a commission at no extra cost to you. We only recommend items that fit the use case described on this page, and we label recommendations based on whether they were tested, handled, or researched.

Required Amazon disclosure if Amazon links are used:

> As an Amazon Associate, Everyday Carry Finder earns from qualifying purchases.

Required TSA disclaimer on TSA/travel-sensitive pages:

> TSA rules can change, and final decisions at the checkpoint rest with TSA officers. This page is a conservative guide, not a guarantee that an item will be allowed through security. Always check the latest TSA guidance before you travel.

Required knife/local law disclaimer where knives or tool legality are discussed:

> Knife and tool laws vary by location, workplace, school, venue, and travel context. Check your local rules and policies before carrying a knife or tool.

Operational rules:

- Do not display exact Amazon prices unless compliant with current Amazon Associates requirements and API usage.
- Do not use Amazon product images unless compliant with current Amazon Associates requirements and approved API/feed usage.
- Do not make absolute TSA, legal, workplace, or school-policy claims.
- Date-stamp product verification.
- Remove or replace unavailable products.
