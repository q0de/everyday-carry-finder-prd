## Interpretation Rules

- If Reddit and YouTube show repeated buyer questions, prioritize page briefs that answer those exact problems.
- If TikTok/Instagram show high save/share behavior for pouches, slings, or organized kits, emphasize visual systems and "ready to go" layouts in content.
- If Pinterest validates checklists and prepared-lifestyle systems, strengthen the mise-en-place framing on checklist and beginner pages.
- If Meta ads show durable active campaigns for wallets, key organizers, pouches, lights, or slings, prioritize direct-affiliate research in those categories.
- If social demand clusters around personas, consider future Phase B pages like software engineer EDC, dad EDC, commuter EDC, or office-safe no-knife kit.
- If `#edc` pollution makes TikTok unusable, use narrower hashtags and search phrases rather than treating that as weak demand.

## Current Working Takeaway

The early research direction supports "prepared without overpacking" as the best cross-platform angle.

The product should not be just a gear blog. It needs beginner paths, context filters, and category pages like TSA-safe EDC, under-$100 EDC kit, keychain EDC, and office-safe carry.
